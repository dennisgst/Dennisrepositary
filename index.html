Week 7: Electronics

Introduction

An electronic circuit consits mostly of an input signal, a trigger, and an output.

Conductivity

An atom consits of electrons, protons and the nucleus. Electrons are positively charged, Protons are negatively charged and neutrons are not charged at all.

Resistors

These are the simplest components in any circuit. Their job is to restrict the flow of electrons and reduce the current or voltage flowing by converting electrical energy into heat. Resistors come in many different shapes and sizes. Variable resistors have a dial control on them so they change the amount of resistance when you turn them.  

Breadboard

Breadboards are an essential tool for prototyping and building temporary circuits.  These boards contain holes for inserting wire and components.  Because of their temporary nature, they allow you to create circuits without soldering.  The holes in a breadboard are connected in rows both horizontally and vertically.

Voltage

Voltage is the measured "pressure" in which the charged electrons are pressed through. Charge flows through a conductor as an electrical current.

Rules of Electricity

- Electrons are lazy, they always choose the shortest way. 
- Electricity hates leftovers, curcuits use exactly the electrical energy that is used.
- Curcuits are a system. They connect the components in the right arrangement
